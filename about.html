about

I’m a content strategist, writer, and editor currently based in Salt Lake City, Utah.

It would be safe to say I have a passion for content—both consuming and creating it. My career began with communications, copywriting, and editorial work. I worked for magazines, academic journals, and newspapers, often working within the space of traditional print media. But I always wanted to push it further. Get out of ink and into code.

While still working in more traditional communications roles, I developed my web content strategy skills, learning HTML and CSS (and coding this website), researching SEO and accessibility best practices, and delving into UX and information architecture. Not long after, I turned my part-time hustle into a full-time job as the lead copywriter and content strategist at Westminster College.

My diverse professional background has given me an appreciation for the marriage of strategic vision, analytics, and creativity in content strategy. I believe that web work is inherently collaborative—the less we silo our work, the better the user experience will be.

For fun, I co-founded a minimalist fashion blog—although we talk about a lot more than just fashion—and love exploring aesthetics, from fashion and art to architecture and graphic design. I also enjoy staying up all night reading (naturally), trying not to kill my sourdough starter (Carl), traveling as often as possible, spending as much time outside as I can, and, let’s be honest, bingeing on whatever new show is on my radar.
